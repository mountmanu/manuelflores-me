# Measure What Matters
**Author:** John Doerr | **Category:** OKRs / Goal Setting / Management

## Core Thesis
OKRs (Objectives and Key Results) create organizational alignment and accountability by forcing teams to publicly commit to a small number of ambitious, measurable goals — separating aspirational thinking from the metrics that prove progress actually happened.

## Key Frameworks
- **OKRs (Objectives and Key Results):** An Objective is a qualitative, inspiring destination ("what"); Key Results are 3-5 quantitative, time-bound metrics that prove you arrived ("how"). Objectives motivate; KRs measure.
- **CFRs (Conversations, Feedback, Recognition):** The human complement to OKRs — replacing annual reviews with continuous lightweight check-ins, real-time bidirectional feedback, and peer recognition tied to goal progress.
- **Stretch Goals ("Moonshots"):** OKRs are designed to be uncomfortable — a score of 0.7/1.0 is healthy. Consistently hitting 1.0 means goals aren't ambitious enough. Stretch targets force prioritization over execution of the obvious.
- **Committed vs. Aspirational OKRs:** Committed OKRs must be achieved 100% (operational, non-negotiable). Aspirational OKRs are moonshots where 70% attainment is success. Teams must label which type each OKR is or grading breaks down.
- **Cascading Alignment (Bidirectional):** OKRs should cascade top-down for company alignment, but also bottom-up — individual contributors propose their own OKRs that roll up to team objectives, creating buy-in and surfacing ground-level insight.

## The 20% That Matters Most
1. **Limit to 3-5 OKRs per cycle.** More than five objectives signals lack of strategic clarity. The discipline of choosing what NOT to track is as valuable as tracking the right things.
2. **Key Results must be binary or numeric — never activity-based.** "Launch three demos" is a task. "Increase demo-to-pilot conversion rate from 30% to 50%" is a Key Result. If you can't score it 0.0 to 1.0, rewrite it.
3. **Make OKRs public and transparent.** Every employee at every level should be able to see every other person's OKRs. Transparency creates peer accountability more powerfully than manager oversight does.
4. **Decouple OKRs from compensation.** Tying OKRs directly to bonuses destroys candor — people sandbag targets to guarantee payouts. Use CFRs and separate performance conversations for compensation decisions.
5. **Cadence matters: run quarterly OKRs inside an annual framework.** Annual OKRs set strategic direction; quarterly OKRs create the urgency and correction loops needed in fast-moving environments. Weekly check-ins (confidence ratings, blockers) keep them alive.
6. **Score ruthlessly, but grade with context.** A 0.4 on an aspirational OKR with a strong postmortem is more valuable than a sandbagged 1.0. The debrief after each cycle — what caused the score — is where learning compounds.
7. **Use OKRs to say no.** The primary operational value of OKRs is making it organizationally safe to decline requests that fall outside the committed goals. If a new initiative doesn't map to an OKR, it requires explicit leadership sign-off to pursue.
8. **Continuous feedback (CFRs) is the engine that makes OKRs move.** Goals set in January and reviewed in December are theater. Managers should hold brief weekly or biweekly check-ins focused on: progress against KRs, blockers to remove, and one piece of specific behavioral feedback.

## Key Quotes
> "Ideas are easy. Execution is everything. It takes a team to win."

> "A goal without a Key Result is just a wish."

> "The single most important sentence in goal setting is: I will [Objective] as measured by [Key Results]."

## SE/Presales Application
SEs should define personal OKRs around the metrics they actually control — technical win rate, time-to-POC completion, champion-enablement activities per account — rather than inheriting vague quota-support targets from sales. Using CFR-style check-ins with AE partners (weekly deal reviews with explicit blockers and feedback) replaces the reactive "what do you need from me?" dynamic with a structured, accountable co-selling motion.
