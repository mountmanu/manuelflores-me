# Sell the Way You Buy
**Author:** David Priemer | **Category:** Modern Sales / Buyer Psychology

## Core Thesis
Sellers are buyers too — and modern buyers are overwhelmed, skeptical, and self-serve. The most effective sellers stop "selling" and start helping buyers buy by understanding and removing friction in the buying journey.

## Key Frameworks
- **The Buyer's Journey ≠ The Sales Process:** Your CRM stages describe your internal process; buyers follow their own emotional and logical journey. Map to theirs.
- **The Three Customer Emotions:** Hope (what could be), Fear (what could go wrong), Regret (what they missed). Effective sales messaging triggers hope while reducing fear.
- **The Emotional Why:** Features don't move people — emotions do. Always answer "how will this make the buyer feel?"
- **The Diagnostic Mindset:** Approach every interaction as a doctor would — diagnose before prescribing.

## The 20% That Matters Most
1. Your pitch sounds very different to a buyer than it does to you. Test it with people outside your company.
2. Prospects don't care about your product — they care about their problems. Lead with their world, not yours.
3. Modern buyers do 60-70% of their research before contacting sales. By the time they talk to you, they have opinions.
4. "Always be helping" replaces "always be closing." Help first; the sale follows.
5. Objections late in the process signal that early conversations failed to address unstated concerns.
6. Empathy is a competitive advantage — sellers who genuinely understand buyer fears outperform those who don't.
7. The best demo shows the buyer their world, not your product's features.
8. Social proof from similar companies is more persuasive than any ROI calculator.

## Key Quotes
> "Think about the last time you enjoyed being sold to. That's what you should aim for."
> "Your best sales process is the one that makes buying easiest."

## SE/Presales Application
Directly applicable to demo design and discovery framing. The "buyer's journey" lens helps SEs understand why a prospect who seemed enthusiastic in a demo goes cold — they didn't feel understood.
