# Doing Discovery
**Author:** Peter Cohan | **Category:** Presales / Sales Discovery

## Core Thesis
Discovery is the most underdeveloped and highest-leverage skill in enterprise sales. Most sellers gather symptoms; great sellers diagnose root causes and quantify business impact — turning discovery into the first act of value delivery.

## Key Frameworks
- **The Five Levels of Discovery:** Situation → Problem → Impact → Critical Business Issues → Decision Criteria. Most SEs stop at level 2.
- **Quantified Business Value (QBV):** Every pain must be attached to a number. "We lose deals" becomes "we lose 3 of 10 deals in the final stage, each worth ~$200K, costing us $600K/quarter."
- **The SIBME Model:** Situation → Issue → Business Impact → Metric → Evidence. Structure every discovery finding this way.
- **The "Three Whys":** Drill into any stated problem three levels deep before accepting it as the real problem.

## The 20% That Matters Most
1. The buyer's stated problem is almost never the real problem — ask "why does that matter to you?" repeatedly.
2. Quantify impact in the customer's own language and numbers, not yours. Let them do the math.
3. Discovery should feel like a value-adding conversation, not an interrogation. Give insights in return.
4. Distinguish between "nice to have" pains and "critical business issues" — only CBIs drive buying decisions.
5. Great discovery questions: "What happens if you don't solve this?" and "How are you solving this today?"
6. Document discovery findings and play them back before demoing — this is both a confirmation and a commitment.
7. Discovery never ends — continue discovering through demos, POCs, and negotiation.
8. The best discovery uncovers pains the buyer didn't know they could articulate.

## Key Quotes
> "Discovery is the most neglected skill in sales, and the one with the highest ROI."
> "Asking 'why' three times will reveal the real problem 80% of the time."

## SE/Presales Application
Pairs directly with "Great Demo!" (same author). Use the SIBME framework to structure pre-call discovery notes and post-call summaries. The QBV concept is what turns a technical win into a business justification for purchase.
