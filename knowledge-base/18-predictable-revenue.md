# Predictable Revenue
**Author:** Aaron Ross & Marylou Tyler | **Category:** SaaS Sales / Revenue Operations

## Core Thesis
Cold calling is dead — scalable outbound revenue comes from a specialized, process-driven prospecting system (Cold Calling 2.0) where distinct roles (prospectors, closers, farmers) each do only one job, generating a predictable pipeline that compounds over time like a machine.

## Key Frameworks
- **Cold Calling 2.0:** Outbound prospecting without cold calls — instead, send short "referral request" emails to executives asking who the right person to talk to is, then convert warm referrals into first calls. Eliminates rejection and scales.
- **The Three Revenue Levers (Seeds, Nets, Spears):** Seeds = word-of-mouth/customer success revenue (slow but high-value); Nets = inbound marketing (wide catch, variable quality); Spears = targeted outbound prospecting (precise, controllable, scalable). Only Spears are truly predictable and manageable.
- **Specialization of Sales Roles:** Split the sales function into three distinct roles — Market Response Reps (inbound SDRs), Outbound SDRs (Cold Calling 2.0 prospectors), and Account Executives (closers). No rep should both prospect and close.
- **The Predictable Revenue Equation:** Pipeline = (# of qualified opportunities per month) x (avg deal size) x (close rate). To grow revenue predictably, instrument each variable separately rather than asking AEs to "work harder."
- **Success Metrics Ladder:** Track activity metrics (emails sent) → result metrics (responses, referrals) → pipeline metrics (SQLs created) → revenue metrics (closed-won). Each layer must be healthy before the next one matters.

## The 20% That Matters Most
1. **Separate prospecting from closing.** The single most impactful structural change in any sales org: never ask an AE to cold prospect. Dedicated SDRs ramp faster, hit quota more consistently, and free AEs to do what they're best at — running deals.
2. **Use "referral email" prospecting, not cold pitches.** A 3-5 sentence email to a VP asking "who on your team handles X?" gets a 10-30% response rate. The subject line should be the prospect's company name — nothing more. This is the core Cold Calling 2.0 motion.
3. **Target 5-10 accounts deeply, not 100 accounts broadly.** Outbound SDRs should send targeted, personalized emails to a narrow Ideal Customer Profile rather than blasting large lists. Volume is the enemy of quality at the top of funnel.
4. **Qualify rigorously before handing off to AEs.** An SQL (Sales Qualified Lead) must have confirmed Pain, Authority, Money, and Timeline. Passing bad leads to AEs destroys morale and distorts pipeline forecasts.
5. **Build a lead-gen function before scaling AEs.** Hiring more closers before fixing top-of-funnel is the most common and costly mistake. One SDR can feed 2-3 AEs; establish that ratio before growing headcount.
6. **Make revenue predictable by measuring pipeline creation rate, not just bookings.** If you know how many SQLs are generated per SDR per month and your historical close rate, you can model revenue 60-90 days out. Forecasting accuracy is a system property, not a talent property.
7. **Customer Success is the highest-ROI revenue lever.** Expansion revenue from existing customers (Seeds) is 5-7x cheaper to generate than net-new. A dedicated Customer Success function — not AEs doing renewals — is required to unlock this reliably.
8. **Ramp time is predictable — plan for it.** A new SDR takes 2-3 months to ramp; a new AE takes 4-6 months. A CEO expecting new hires to produce revenue in month one will always be disappointed. Build hiring lead times into the revenue model explicitly.

## SE/Presales Application
SEs sit at the handoff between SDR-qualified pipeline and closed revenue — which means they are the most expensive point of pipeline waste when AEs receive unqualified leads. Understanding the Cold Calling 2.0 qualification criteria (Pain, Authority, Money, Timeline) lets an SE quickly diagnose at discovery whether a deal is real, redirect scope if it isn't, and avoid investing demo/POC cycles in accounts that were never going to close.
