# Fanatical Prospecting
**Author:** Jeb Blount | **Category:** Sales / Prospecting / Pipeline

## Core Thesis
An empty pipeline is the single greatest threat to a sales career, and the cure is fanatical, daily, multi-channel prospecting — done with urgency — because no technique, tool, or talent compensates for a failure to consistently fill the top of the funnel.

## Key Frameworks
- **The 30-Day Rule:** Prospecting activity done in any 30-day window determines your pipeline and income 90 days later — the lag means pain is always delayed, so top performers prospect even when busy.
- **The Three Ps (Mindset Blockers):** Procrastination, Perfectionism, and Paralysis are the three psychological traps that kill prospecting discipline; recognizing them is the first step to defeating them.
- **The Telephone Is King:** Despite the rise of email and social, the phone remains the highest-leverage prospecting tool for speed and human connection — Blount argues most reps dramatically underuse it out of fear.
- **The Prospecting Pyramid:** Outbound channels stack in priority — phone at the top, then in-person, email, social selling, text, and referrals — with each channel serving a different role in warming and converting prospects.
- **Golden Hours / Platinum Hours:** Protect your highest-energy, business-hours time blocks exclusively for live prospecting activity; administrative and CRM work belong in off-peak hours (early morning, evenings), never during prime selling time.

## The 20% That Matters Most
1. **Prospect every single day, no exceptions.** Top performers block dedicated prospecting time on their calendar as a non-negotiable appointment — even one missed week creates a 90-day pipeline hole that is painful to recover from.
2. **Own your numbers.** Calculate your personal conversion ratios (dials → conversations → meetings → opportunities → closed deals) so you know exactly how many touches you need daily to hit quota — then execute that math mechanically.
3. **Phone first, always.** A single live phone conversation is worth 10 cold emails; call every prospect before layering in other channels. Fear of the phone is the number-one pipeline killer among modern reps.
4. **Use the Interruption Paradigm.** Cold calls and cold emails are interruptions — your only job in the first 5 seconds is to avoid the delete/hang-up reflex with a relevant, punchy opener that earns 30 more seconds, not to pitch the whole solution.
5. **Leverage referrals systematically, not occasionally.** A referred prospect converts at 3-5x the rate of a cold one — build a weekly habit of explicitly asking satisfied customers, colleagues, and partners for introductions by name, not just "anyone you know."
6. **Batch prospecting activity into blocks.** Context-switching destroys momentum; batch 1-2 hour blocks of pure outbound (phone only, email only, LinkedIn only) to maximize efficiency and reduce call reluctance.
7. **Use social selling to warm, not to replace.** LinkedIn and other social platforms are best used to research, comment, and create familiarity before the call — treating social as a substitute for the phone is a rationalization, not a strategy.
8. **Message discipline: WIIFM every time.** Every prospecting message must immediately answer the prospect's subconscious question "What's In It For Me?" — lead with a business outcome relevant to their role and industry, never with your product features or company background.

## Key Quotes

> "You must adopt the mindset that prospecting is not optional. It is not something you do when you have time. It is something you do every day, without question, without fail."

> "Telephone prospecting is the most disruptive, most powerful, and most effective prospecting methodology available to salespeople today — and the most avoided."

## SE/Presales Application
SEs who proactively source their own pipeline — by calling past champions, following up on stalled deals, and reaching out to new personas in existing accounts — close more deals and have stronger standing with AEs than those who wait passively for demos to land in their queue. Applying the 30-Day Rule and Golden Hours discipline to outreach (e.g., calling a champion who went dark, pinging a new economic buyer at an installed-base account) turns presales from a reactive function into a revenue-generating one.
