# New Strategic Selling
**Author:** Robert Miller & Stephen Heiman | **Category:** Enterprise Sales / Account Strategy

## Core Thesis
Every complex B2B sale involves multiple buyers with different roles, agendas, and levels of influence — and a deal is only winnable if you identify, map, and satisfy each of them before the decision is made.

## Key Frameworks
- **Four Buying Influences:** Every enterprise deal has four roles — Economic Buyer (final budget authority), User Buyer (uses the product day-to-day), Technical Buyer (screens for fit/risk/compliance), and Coach (insider who guides you and wants you to win). You must reach all four or the deal has blind spots.
- **Response Modes:** Buyers sit in one of four psychological states toward change — Growth (open, pulling you in), Trouble (reactive, urgent), Even Keel (complacent, hard to move), or Overconfident (dismissive). Your entry strategy must match their mode; rational product pitches fail on Even Keel or Overconfident buyers.
- **Win-Results:** A "Win" is what the organization gets (ROI, reduced risk, efficiency); a "Result" is what the individual buyer personally gets (recognition, career safety, removal of a pain they own). Both must be delivered — Result without Win loses the deal politically; Win without Result loses the individual.
- **The Funnel / Sales Process Stages:** Miller Heiman maps pipeline to concrete verifiable outcomes at each stage — not activity milestones but confirmed buyer actions — forcing reps to distinguish "where I think I am" from "what the buyer has actually done."
- **Strengths/Vulnerabilities Analysis (Blue Sheet):** A structured account review tool that scores your position on each buying influence, your competitive strength, and gaps — turning gut-feel into an explicit plan with specific actions.

## The 20% That Matters Most
1. **Map all four buying influences before your first demo.** Most SEs get pulled into technical evaluations by a User Buyer or Technical Buyer without ever confirming who the Economic Buyer is. If you haven't confirmed economic authority, you're building a case no one with budget will see.
2. **Coach access is non-negotiable.** A Coach is not a champion who likes you — it's someone with credibility inside the account who actively shares information about internal politics, competing priorities, and decision criteria. Without one you are flying blind; find or develop one in every deal.
3. **Diagnose Response Mode before you pitch.** Presenting ROI and efficiency to an Even Keel buyer (no perceived problem) will be met with "interesting, we'll keep you in mind." Trigger movement by helping them see a gap between where they are and where they need to be — not by describing your product.
4. **Separate Win from Result, then address both explicitly.** When a VP of Engineering says "we need faster deployments," that's the organizational Win. Their personal Result might be "I stop being the bottleneck my CTO complains about." Tie your solution to both in every executive conversation.
5. **Red flags are non-negotiable stop signs.** Miller Heiman defines specific red flags — too much access to only one contact, a sponsor who "will handle the other stakeholders," a deal that came in unsolicited with no history. Each red flag is a signal you lack coverage, and you must address it with a specific action or disqualify.
6. **Never rely on a single coach or champion.** Organizations reorganize, champions leave, and political winds shift. If your entire access to an account runs through one person, you are one departure away from a dead deal. Continuously expand your internal network.
7. **Distinguish "best" from "best for this account."** Strategic Selling explicitly warns against pitching your ideal solution — pitch the solution that best fits this buyer's specific Wins and Results. Overselling features the buyer didn't ask for creates Technical Buyer resistance and erodes trust.
8. **Consistent use of the Blue Sheet forces honesty.** The discipline of filling out a structured account review before every major customer meeting reveals coverage gaps you would otherwise rationalize away. Teams that use it systematically close more forecasted deals because they act on reality, not optimism.

## Key Quotes
> "You can't manage a sale you don't understand. The Blue Sheet is not a form — it is a mirror."

> "Every buying influence has a personal Win. If you don't know what it is, you don't know why they should say yes."

> "A Champion who can't or won't give you access to the Economic Buyer is not a Champion — they are a blocker wearing a friendly face."

## SE/Presales Application
As a Sales Engineer, you are almost always introduced as the Technical Buyer's counterpart — but your strategic leverage lies in recognizing which of the four buying influences you are actually talking to and adjusting your demo, discovery, and proof points accordingly. The highest-value SE move is to use technical conversations to surface personal Results for each stakeholder (reduced operational risk, faster onboarding, less support burden) and feed that intelligence back to the AE so the full buying committee is covered before a POC or proposal lands.
