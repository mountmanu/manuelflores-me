# Amp It Up
**Author:** Frank Slootman | **Category:** Leadership / Execution / Scaling

## Core Thesis
Average companies accept average performance. Exceptional execution comes from relentlessly raising standards, increasing urgency, and narrowing focus — not from adding resources or complexity.

## Key Frameworks
- **The Three Levers:** Raise standards + Increase urgency + Narrow focus. Applied simultaneously, they compound.
- **The "So What?" Standard:** Every meeting, memo, and decision must pass the "so what?" test — what changes because of this? If nothing changes, don't do it.
- **Tempo as Strategy:** Speed is itself a competitive advantage. Faster iterations, faster decisions, faster hiring. Slow companies lose not on quality but on clock speed.
- **Specificity of Mission:** Vague missions ("be the best") create vague execution. Narrow, specific, measurable missions create alignment.

## The 20% That Matters Most
1. Most companies are operating at 60-70% of their potential — urgency and standards are the levers, not more headcount.
2. Leaders who tolerate mediocrity become mediocre. The standard you walk past is the standard you set.
3. "No" is a strategic tool. Slootman's superpower at ServiceNow was saying no to almost everything to do a few things exceptionally.
4. Move faster than feels comfortable — the right pace for most organizations is the one that creates slight anxiety.
5. Hire for trajectory and horsepower, not just experience. People who've already arrived often stop moving.
6. The CEO's primary job is to set and maintain standards, then hold the organization accountable to them relentlessly.
7. Accountability without authority is frustrating; authority without accountability is dangerous. Match them.
8. Don't manage consensus — drive conviction and then move.

## Key Quotes
> "Most companies are a sleeping giant. They just need someone to amp it up."
> "Urgency is not a management style — it's a mindset. Either you have it or you don't."
> "The world belongs to those who can execute."

## SE/Presales Application
Useful for understanding how high-performing sales orgs operate from the top down. Helps SEs understand why best-in-class companies have rigorous deal processes and why "we need to move faster" from AEs is cultural, not just pressure.
