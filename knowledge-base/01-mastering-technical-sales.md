# Mastering Technical Sales
**Authors:** John Care & Aron Bohlig | **Category:** Presales / SE

## Core Thesis
The Sales Engineer is a business professional who happens to be technical — not a technician who happens to sell. SE success is about guiding buyers through complexity toward confident decisions, not showcasing features.

## Key Frameworks
- **The SE Success Triangle:** Technical skills + Sales skills + Business acumen. Most SEs over-index on technical; the differentiator is business acumen.
- **The Four Levels of Value:** Feature/Function → Business Value → Political Value → Personal Value. Demos that stay at level 1 lose deals.
- **The 70/20/10 Rule for Demos:** 70% discovery-driven (show what they asked for), 20% value proof, 10% wow factor — never the reverse.
- **The POC Trap:** A proof of concept requested without clear success criteria is a free consulting engagement. Always get written criteria before starting.

## The 20% That Matters Most
1. Discovery before demos — never show a product without understanding the buyer's top 3 pain points and business impact.
2. Great SEs ask "what does success look like for you?" before any technical deep-dive.
3. The "So What?" test: after every feature you plan to show, ask yourself "so what does this mean for the customer?" If you can't answer, cut it.
4. Champion enablement: your internal champion needs to be able to sell you internally without you present. Give them the language.
5. Technical objections are usually political objections in disguise. Dig for the real concern.
6. Qualify POCs ruthlessly: Who owns it? What are the success criteria? What happens if you pass?
7. Manage the "technical win" separately from the "business win" — you can win technically and lose the deal.
8. SE metrics that matter: demo-to-POC conversion rate, POC win rate, time-to-technical-win.

## Key Quotes
> "The job of the SE is to reduce technical risk and increase business confidence."
> "A feature without a benefit is just noise."
> "Never demo anything you haven't agreed matters to the customer."
