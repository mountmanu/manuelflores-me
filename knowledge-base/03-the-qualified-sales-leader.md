# The Qualified Sales Leader
**Author:** John McMahon | **Category:** Enterprise Sales / Sales Leadership

## Core Thesis
Winning in enterprise sales requires a rigorous, systematic qualification framework applied at every stage of the pipeline — not optimism or gut feel. The best sales leaders build repeatable processes that surface truth quickly and eliminate wishful thinking from forecasts.

## Key Frameworks
- **MEDDPICC:** Metrics, Economic Buyer, Decision Criteria, Decision Process, Paper Process, Identify Pain, Champion, Competition. The gold standard qualification framework for enterprise deals.
- **The Champion Test:** A true champion has power/influence, actively sells on your behalf when you're not in the room, and has personal gain tied to the project succeeding.
- **Pain ≠ Problem:** Buyers have latent pains constantly — they only buy when the pain is severe enough to justify disruption. Your job is to increase the perceived cost of inaction.
- **The Three Columns:** Every sales rep operates in one of three states — over-achiever, achiever, or underachiever. Identify early, coach to achievers, move on from persistent underachievers.

## The 20% That Matters Most
1. MEDDPICC is not a checklist — it's a live diagnostic. Gaps in any letter = gaps in your deal.
2. "No champion, no deal." If you can't name who is selling for you internally, you don't have a champion.
3. Forecast accuracy is a lagging indicator of qualification discipline — fix qualification, forecasts fix themselves.
4. The Economic Buyer (EB) often isn't the person you're talking to. Map to the EB early or lose late.
5. Decision criteria you don't know are decision criteria you can't influence — always ask "how will you decide?"
6. Pain must be tied to metrics for a deal to be real. "We want to improve X" is not a pain; "X costs us $Y per quarter" is.
7. The best sales reps discover problems; average reps pitch solutions to stated requirements.
8. Competition is always present — even "no decision" is a competitor. Always ask "what happens if you do nothing?"

## Key Quotes
> "Hope is not a strategy. Pipeline is not revenue."
> "If you can't identify the economic buyer, you're not selling — you're presenting."
> "Weak qualification is the root cause of almost every forecast miss."

## SE/Presales Application
MEDDPICC shapes how SEs should approach discovery and POC scoping. Before designing any POC, ensure Metrics, Pain, and Decision Criteria are documented. This is how SEs become deal qualifiers, not just demo givers.
