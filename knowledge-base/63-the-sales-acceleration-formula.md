# The Sales Acceleration Formula
**Author:** Mark Roberge | **Category:** SaaS Sales / Revenue Operations / Hiring

## Core Thesis
Predictable, scalable revenue growth is an engineering problem, not an art form — you build it by applying data, science, and repeatable processes to hiring, training, managing, and generating demand, replacing gut-feel management with measurable systems.

## Key Frameworks
- **The Sales Hiring Formula:** Define the specific attributes that predict success at your company (Roberge used five: coachability, curiosity, prior success, intelligence, work ethic) and score every candidate against them to remove bias from hiring decisions.
- **The Sales Training Formula:** New reps should not carry a quota until they can pass a certification exam that mirrors the real buyer journey — covering persona knowledge, pain points, competitive landscape, and the product demo itself.
- **The Sales Coaching Formula:** Managers inspect one specific metric per rep per week (e.g., call-to-connect rate, demo-to-proposal rate) rather than reviewing overall quota attainment; coaching is tied to the single biggest constraint in each rep's funnel.
- **The Sales Management Formula (SBI — Sales by the Numbers):** Build a quantitative model that ties hiring plan, ramp time, quota, and attrition rate to a revenue target, making headcount decisions as precise as a financial forecast.
- **The Demand Generation Formula:** Apply the same scientific rigor to inbound/outbound — test a small cohort of a new channel, measure lead-to-close rate and cost per acquired customer, then scale only what clears the threshold.

## The 20% That Matters Most
1. Define your Ideal Customer Profile (ICP) with empirical data before optimizing anything else — Roberge analyzed closed-won deals at HubSpot and found company size, industry, and existing budget for marketing were the three strongest predictors of success; your hiring, messaging, and lead scoring must all derive from this ICP.
2. Hire for coachability above all other traits. A rep who receives feedback, adjusts, and improves compounds in value; a rep who resists feedback plateaus regardless of raw talent.
3. New reps should spend their first four weeks learning the buyer, not the product — passing the buyer-journey certification before touching a prospect ensures they can lead discovery with empathy, not pitch reflexes.
4. The sales manager's job is to be a coach, not a closer. When a manager takes over a call to save a deal, they hide the rep's skill gap instead of closing it; structured role-play debriefs build the rep faster than deal rescues.
5. Track leading indicators (connect rate, demo completion rate, proposal-sent rate) by rep and by cohort — lagging indicators like quota attainment tell you what happened four months ago; leading indicators tell you what will happen next quarter.
6. Compensation design is strategy: at HubSpot, paying commission on monthly recurring revenue (MRR) rather than total contract value aligned reps with customer success and reduced churn-inducing deals.
7. Sales and Marketing alignment requires a shared definition of a "qualified lead" — without a written SLA specifying what Marketing hands off and what Sales will do within 24 hours, each team optimizes for their own metric at the expense of the pipeline.
8. Scale what survives the cohort test: run any new channel, message, or persona as a structured 90-day experiment with a small group, measure cost-per-acquired-customer against LTV, and only roll out broadly when the economics are proven.

## Key Quotes
> "I never hired a sales rep. I administered a test and hired the person who scored highest."

> "The best sales managers are coaches, not closers. The moment a manager jumps in to save a deal, they rob the rep of the learning that would have made the next ten deals winnable."

> "If you can't define what a good salesperson looks like in your company before you hire, you'll never be able to replicate it."

## SE/Presales Application
SEs are hired and evaluated with even less rigor than AEs — applying Roberge's scoring rubric (define the 5 attributes of a great SE at your company, score candidates against them, certify on buyer journey before they join a live deal) removes the "I know a good SE when I see one" bias that leads to inconsistent presales quality. The coaching formula is equally direct: track each SE's demo-to-technical-win rate and POC-to-close rate as leading indicators, then coach the one bottleneck metric per SE per sprint rather than reviewing overall quota coverage.
