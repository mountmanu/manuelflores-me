# Phase 3 — Outreach

Drafting messages to recruiters, hiring managers, warm-intro contacts, or follow-ups on ghosted threads. Manuel already runs a Warm-First Outreach System (33 companies, 37 named decision-makers). This phase produces individual messages for that system or for new opportunities.

## The five outreach types

Identify which one the user is asking for. Each has a different shape.

| Type | Use when | Length cap |
|---|---|---|
| **Warm intro** | Manuel has a mutual connection or has engaged the target's content; he is asking for a 15-min call or an intro to a hiring manager | ≤120 words |
| **Cold hiring-manager DM** | No mutual connection; direct LinkedIn or email to the hiring manager | ≤120 words |
| **Recruiter response** | Recruiter has reached out OR Manuel has applied and a recruiter has replied; Manuel needs to respond | ≤150 words |
| **Follow-up on silence** | 5–10 business days have passed with no response; one polite ping | ≤60 words |
| **Post-interview thank-you** | Sent within 24 hours of any interview round | ≤100 words |

## Universal principles

These apply to every outreach type. No exceptions.

### 1. Lead with relevance, not introduction

Never open with "I am Manuel Flores, founder of Areté Soluciones." Open with the relevance hook — the specific reason this message is landing in their inbox right now.

- ❌ "Hi Sarah, I'm Manuel Flores, a Sales Engineer based in Mexico, and I'm reaching out about your open SE role..."
- ✅ "Hi Sarah — saw your team is hiring an SE to anchor LATAM expansion. I'm a bilingual EN/ES native SE who has shipped 11 production B2B SaaS systems to Mexican enterprise from my own company. Three-minute pitch?"

### 2. Quantify in the first two sentences

Every outreach should contain at least one number in the opening: revenue, deployments, FPS, accounts, years, percentages. Numbers compress information and signal seriousness.

### 3. Reference something specific to the target

A piece of content they wrote, a hire they just made, a customer logo they just announced, a job posting they wrote in Spanish. This proves the message is not blast outreach.

### 4. Make the ask explicit and small

The ask is always a 15-minute call or a specific introduction. Never "let me know if you'd like to chat" — too vague. Always "would you have 15 minutes on Tuesday or Thursday next week?"

### 5. Sign off with one credibility anchor

End with a single line that points to one piece of verifiable credibility — usually the Embedded Vision Summit, the Areté URL, or a specific case study link. Do not stack credentials in the signature.

## Templates

These are starting points, not literal scripts. Adapt to the specific target.

### Warm intro template

```
Hi [Name] —

[Specific reference: thing they posted, hire they made, customer they just announced, podcast they were on, etc.] caught my attention because [why it connects to Manuel's profile].

Quick context on me: bilingual EN/ES native SE, founder of Areté Soluciones (Mexican B2B SaaS firm, 11+ production deployments, ~$75K cumulative revenue), recently selected to demo Edge AI live at Embedded Vision Summit Santa Clara May 2026.

[Specific ask: intro to hiring manager X / 15-min call about role Y / opinion on whether my profile fits Z.] Tuesday or Thursday next week work?

— Manuel
manuelflores.me
```

### Cold hiring-manager DM template

```
Hi [Name] —

Saw [Company]'s SE/SC opening for [LATAM / AI infra / specific territory]. My profile is unusually specific to this:

— Bilingual EN/ES native, US-Mexico dual citizen
— Founder of Areté Soluciones, 11+ production B2B SaaS deployments to Mexican enterprise, $75K+ cumulative revenue
— Selected to demo live Edge AI inference at Embedded Vision Summit (Santa Clara, May 2026)
— MEDDIC/Challenger/Command of the Message operational, not aspirational

15 minutes next week to compare notes on your LATAM thesis?

— Manuel
manuelflores.me · linkedin.com/in/manuel-flores-90653060
```

### Recruiter response template

```
Hi [Recruiter] —

Thanks for reaching out. Quick yes — this role fits the profile I'm targeting (SE/SC at [type of company], remote-LATAM, ≥$130K total comp). Open dates this week and next: [list 3–4 windows].

Couple of upfront items so we don't waste cycles:

1. Comp expectation: $[X]K base + variable / RSUs, USD-denominated, paid via [EOR / 1099 / W-2 if relocating]. Open to discuss but want to flag early.
2. Location: Mexico, fully remote. Time zone overlaps US Eastern through Pacific.
3. Areté Soluciones (my company) continues running in maintenance mode — no conflict with [Company], no overlap with your customer base. Happy to walk through structure on the call.

Looking forward.

— Manuel
manuelflores.me
```

### Follow-up on silence template

```
Hi [Name] — bumping this in case it got buried. Still keen on [role / conversation]. If now isn't the right window, no problem — just let me know if I should circle back next quarter. — Manuel
```

### Post-interview thank-you template

```
Hi [Name] —

Thanks for the [time / discovery conversation / demo session] today.

The thing I'm still chewing on: [one specific topic from the conversation that genuinely interested Manuel — a customer pain, a competitive question, a technical tradeoff]. [One sentence with a concrete additional thought or resource — a link, a follow-up question, a "I'll have data on X for you by Friday" commitment.]

Looking forward to next steps.

— Manuel
```

## When the user pastes a recruiter email

Extract:
- Recruiter name + company
- Role title + level
- Any comp band they disclosed
- Any disqualifying gates they mentioned (location, citizenship, equity-only structure)
- Any specific Areté/CV claim they referenced

Then draft the response using the recruiter template above, with the specific extracted details substituted in.

**Always add upfront comp expectation in the response.** Manuel's floor is $130K total comp. Surfacing it in message 1 saves both sides from wasting time on misaligned offers.

## When the user pastes a hiring manager's LinkedIn profile or email

Extract:
- Their name + title + company
- Recent content (posts, comments, talks) — pull 1–2 that signal what they care about
- Tenure at company
- Background (prior companies — anything that overlaps Manuel's experience: Mexican market, AI infra, SE leadership, etc.)

Then draft the cold-DM template above, with the relevance hook drawn from their recent content.

## Bilingual handling

Manuel is fully bilingual. Default language rules:

- **Default to English** for all outreach to US-headquartered companies.
- **Switch to Spanish** if the hiring manager / recruiter is based in LATAM (Mexico, Colombia, Argentina, Chile, Brazil) AND their LinkedIn primary language is Spanish.
- **Send bilingual** (Spanish first, English version below "Translated for convenience:") only on warm-intro messages to LATAM contacts where the relationship is local but the role is US-headquartered.

## What never to do

- Never apologize for cold outreach in the opening line. Wastes words; signals weakness.
- Never include a CV attachment in the first message. Link to manuelflores.me instead.
- Never list more than 4 credentials in any single message. Pick the most relevant 3.
- Never ask "is now a good time to chat?" Propose specific windows.
- Never close with "Looking forward to your response!" — assumes a response. Close with the specific ask and a sign-off.
- Never use emojis. Manuel's prior stated preference.

When asked to draft outreach, output in this shape:

```

RELEVANCE HOOK: [the one specific thing this message references]

MESSAGE:
[the drafted message, ≤word cap for the type]

SEND-OR-WAIT: [send now | wait N days because X | send only after Y]
EXPECTED RESPONSE RATE: [estimated percentage based on outreach type and warmth]
```
